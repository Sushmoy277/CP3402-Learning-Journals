# Week 8 – Learning Experiment

## 1. Hypothesis
I believe that studying in short focused sessions using the Pomodoro technique will help me stay more focused and understand topics better compared to studying for a long time without breaks.

## 2. Experiment Design
To test this, I used two different study methods:

- **Method 1 (Continuous Study):**  
I studied one topic for 1 hour without taking any breaks.

- **Method 2 (Pomodoro Technique):**  
I studied another similar topic using:
  - 25 minutes study  
  - 5 minutes break  
  - repeated twice  

Both topics were from the same subject to make the comparison fair.

## 3. Procedure (What I Did)
1. I selected two similar topics from the CMS subject.  
2. On Day 1, I studied Topic 1 continuously for 1 hour.  
3. On Day 2, I studied Topic 2 using the Pomodoro technique.  
4. After each session, I observed:
   - my focus level  
   - my understanding  
   - how tired I felt  
## 4. Results
- During the 1 hour continuous study, I started losing focus after some time.  
- I felt more tired and distracted towards the end.  
- During the Pomodoro sessions, I stayed focused in each 25-minute session.  
- The short breaks helped me feel refreshed and continue studying better.  
- I understood and remembered the topic more clearly when using the Pomodoro method.  
## 5. Analysis
The results support my hypothesis.

The Pomodoro technique helped me stay focused and reduced mental tiredness. Breaking the study time into smaller parts made it easier to stay engaged.

On the other hand, studying continuously made it harder to stay focused and reduced my overall productivity.
